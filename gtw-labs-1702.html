Core Module

The Model 1702 is designed to be a highly adaptable data acquisition tool enabling collection, storage and transmission of time stamped data from varying sources and protocols. The Model 1702 is a low power consumption device which could be battery powered. This device can be integrated with larger DAQ networks via a multi-drop RS-485 BUS or Bluetooth to a Model 1703 or other gateway module for cloud connectivity.

Key Features

Multi-protocol

  • RS-485 / Modbus
  • 16-bit ADC with 100K SPS
  • Encrypted Bluetooth low energy connectivity

Local Storage with Accurate Timestamp

  • Data can be stored locally on SD-card
  • On-board RTC provides high accurate timestamping

Power

  • Ultra-low power consumption
  • Battery powered or 9-34 VDC

Typical Specifications

System

Microcontroller: Cortex M4F
Clock Speed: 68MHz
Storage: MicroSD Card
Battery backed Real Time Clock

Operating Environment

(-4 to 158)°F & (5 to 95)% Relative Humidity, non-condensing

Power

Requirement: 9-34 VDC or 5VDC or 3.3VDC [DC Power Supply or Battery]
Power Consumption : 1mA @ 24V, 2mA @ 12V, 5mA @ 3.3V, 5mA @ 5V, 0.05mA @ Sleep
Protection: Reverse Polarity, ESD/Voltage Spike & Fuse protections

I/O

1 x Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE)
1 x I²C
1 x RS-485 (ASCII or Modbus)
4 x 0~5V 16bit ADC with 100K SPS
1 x 0~3.3V 12bit ADC with 200K SPS
5 x GPIO

Cloud

Network Connectivity: RS-485 or Bluetooth (Low Energy)
Platform Compatibility: GTWs Hx Monitoring System, Predix IO, or custom cloud services
